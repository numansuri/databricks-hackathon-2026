# Specialist Placement Recommender — Explained

**What it does:** When a specialist signs up to volunteer, it tells them the few districts where they would close the biggest health gap — and names real clinics there that could host them.

## The simple idea

Every district has a level of **need** for a given kind of doctor (how many mothers, children, or patients with a condition need that care) and a level of **supply** (how many of those specialists already work there).

Where the need is high and the supply is low, a new specialist does the most good. Where the need is already met, one more specialist barely moves the needle.

So the tool does one honest thing: **it lines up need against supply, finds the biggest gaps, and points the doctor there.** Each district gets an impact score from 0 to 100 — 100 means "the worst unmet gap in the country for your specialty."

## How it uses your preferences

You tell it where you're willing to work and in what kind of setting (public hospitals, private, or either). It always respects that:

- **Open** — go anywhere; it shows the highest-impact districts nationwide.
- **Prefer** — you lean toward certain states but stay open. It shows the **best options inside your preferred states first**, then a second list of the **highest-impact gaps nationally** — and clearly flags when those are bigger, so you can decide with eyes open.
- **Fixed** — only the states you named; nothing outside them.

The promise: you always see the best choice **within your stated preferences**, and you're never quietly steered away from a bigger opportunity elsewhere — it's named for you.

## A real example

A pediatrician who'll go anywhere is pointed to **Araria, Bihar — impact 100/100, "critical," no pediatric specialist there today.** The driving needs are spelled out: anaemia, child illness care, child malnutrition, child immunization, infant feeding. That is the single worst child-health gap in the data, and an open-minded pediatrician fills it.

A cardiologist who prefers South India sees the best southern option first (Rajanna Sircilla, Telangana, 73/100) **and** an honest note: the very biggest cardiology gap is South District, Sikkim at 100/100 — "consider widening if you're open to it."

## What it does NOT do — honest limits

- **Need is prevalence-scaled, not a raw headcount.** It reflects how widespread a condition is relative to population, not an exact count of patients waiting.
- **Only 17 need-driven specialties carry a signal** (pediatrics, OB-GYN, cardiology, nutrition, and others tied to real survey data). Ask for a dermatologist and it honestly says there's no need-gap signal rather than guessing.
- **Some top districts are "greenfield"** — the gap is real, but the data lists no suitable host clinic. The tool says so plainly instead of inventing one. Where a credible host exists, it names it.
- **It informs a human decision; it does not assign anyone.** Final placement stays with the foundation and the doctor.
